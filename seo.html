Positioning

Built around the basics Google actually recommends: crawlable pages, useful content, clear structure, local business information, and measurable search performance.

SEO should make your website clearer, faster, and easier to trust.

Most small business websites do not need complicated tricks. They need the basics done properly: page titles that make sense, service pages that match what people search for, a Google Business Profile that is complete, a fast site, working metadata, internal links, schema, and Search Console reporting.

SEO is steady work, not a switch.

Good SEO usually starts with fixing what is unclear or broken, then improving the pages that matter most. Some changes help quickly, while others build over months. The goal is to make your site easier to crawl, easier to understand, and more useful to the people searching for what you offer.

Typical timeline
  • First pass Audit, tracking setup, and technical cleanup
  • Month 1–2 Key page and local SEO improvements
  • Month 3+ Content gaps, stronger internal links, ongoing reporting
  • Ongoing Measure what moved and choose the next useful improvement

What I do not promise.

  • Guaranteed #1 rankings
  • Overnight traffic spikes
  • Spammy backlinks
  • Keyword stuffing
  • Lock-in contracts
  • Confusing reports nobody reads
